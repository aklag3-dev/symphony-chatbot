## 1. HANDOVER PROTOCOLS

### 1.1 Battery Handover
- **Minimum State of Charge (SoC) at handover: 30%.**
- **Recommended: 40%** — gives the next driver enough buffer for a peak-hour Heathrow run without an en-route top-up.
- **Below 20% at handover = automatic £35 "low-battery" deduction** from your handover fee, plus a mandatory charge-card top-up before you leave the vehicle.
- **Below 10%** triggers a Symphony Ops investigation; you may be required to explain why you did not charge during your shift.
- The handover record is captured automatically by the Geotab telematics unit at the moment you tap "End Shift." Disputes must be raised within 30 minutes.

### 1.2 Vehicle Condition Handover
- **Walk-around is mandatory** before tapping "End Shift." Take **8 photos** in the app: front, rear, both sides, all four corners, dashboard (showing SoC and any warning lights), and open boot.
- Remove all personal items, rubbish, and charging cables.
- Interior must be **"show-ready"**: no visible food waste, no liquid spills, no strong odours. A quick detail (interior wipe-down, screen clean, window check) is part of your shift.
- **Reject handover** if you see damage that is NOT logged in the app. Do not sign off a damaged vehicle. The "Reject Handover" button pauses your shift; a Symphony duty manager is paged immediately.

### 1.3 Shift Changeover
- **In-person handovers** at Symphony's Stratford and Brent Cross hubs run every 15 minutes between 06:00 and 22:00.
- **Remote / app-only handovers** are permitted between 22:00 and 06:00 and at any time outside the hubs. Both drivers must complete the app checklist within 5 minutes of each other.
- **Skip-shift (driver no-show):** the vehicle stays with the last-known driver until Symphony Ops reassigns it. A £25 per-hour inconvenience fee may be applied to the absent driver.

### 2.2 Network Rules & Limitations
- **bp pulse:** Idle fee kicks in at 90 min. £0.50/min adds up fast — always move the car when charging completes.
- **Ionity:** Always use the Ionity app. Card-on-terminal is surcharge-bearing and will not be reimbursed.
- **Tesla Supercharger:** Symphony vehicles are **NOT approved to use Tesla Supercharger under the card.** Use only in genuine emergencies (see Section 5).
- **Pod Point:** Some sites charge after 60 min. You are liable for overstay fees — Symphony does not reimburse unauthorised overstays.
- **Allstar Plus app:** install on your phone to view live station availability, start sessions, and view session history. Sign-in = your driver number.

### 2.3 How to Check Network Status
- Open **Allstar Plus** → "Map" → live status (available / in use / offline) refreshes every 60 seconds.
- Symphony Driver App → "Charging" tab shows **Symphony-curated status** including known-offline sites from the last 4 hours.
- Real-time issues: ping **#charging-help** in the Symphony Driver Slack — peer drivers and a Symphony duty engineer monitor it 06:00–00:00.

## 3. VEHICLE CONDITION REPORTING

### 3.1 Pre-Shift Inspection (Mandatory)
- Complete within the **first 15 minutes** of every shift.
- Open Symphony Driver App → "Start Shift" → "Vehicle Inspection."
- Take **6 mandatory photos**: front, rear, driver-side, passenger-side, dashboard, charging port.
- Note any damage ≥ 5 cm (scratch, dent, scuff) on the form. **If you don't log it, you may be liable for it.**

### 3.2 Photo Evidence Requirements
- **Lighting:** daylight or well-lit bay. No flash on reflective surfaces.
- **Scale:** place a coin or Symphony-branded ruler (in the glovebox) next to damage in one photo.
- **Timestamp:** EXIF metadata is verified automatically. Photos older than 30 minutes or with stripped metadata are rejected.
- **Do not edit** photos — the app pipeline preserves the original EXIF plus a SHA-256 hash.

### 3.3 Avoiding Blame for the Previous Driver's Mess
- **If the vehicle is dirty, damaged, or shows a warning light at start of shift:** submit a "Pre-existing Condition" report BEFORE you begin driving. The report is timestamped, geo-tagged, and attaches to the vehicle record.
- Take **3 evidence photos + a 10-second walk-around video** (in-app video is supported).
- The previous shift's driver remains liable for damage logged within 48 hours of their shift end.
- **Damage discovered mid-shift:** stop safely, photograph, submit "Mid-Shift Damage" report. Telematics + your photo timestamp establish the discovery window.

### 3.4 Disputes
- If you believe damage has been wrongly attributed to you, raise a "Liability Dispute" in the app within 72 hours. Symphony Ops reviews telematics, the prior and current handover records, and dashcam footage from the Symphony-fitted camera. Outcome issued within 5 working days.

## 5. EMERGENCY PROCEDURES

### 5.1 Low Battery — e.g. 5% SoC, No Approved Station Nearby
1. **Stop safely** in a legal, well-lit location. Hazard lights on. Lock the vehicle.
2. **Call the Symphony 24/7 line** (+44 20 4538 7700). Quote your driver number, vehicle reg, current postcode, and current SoC%.
3. The duty controller will either:
   - **Dispatch a Symphony mobile charger** (response within 45 min inside the M25, 90 min outside) — free of charge, OR
   - **Authorise a non-approved station** (Section 5.2) if mobile charge isn't feasible.
4. **Do not abandon the vehicle** to walk to a charger. A £250 abandonment fee applies and the vehicle is recovered at your cost.
5. After resolution, submit an "Emergency Charge" report in the app within 24 hours with the controller's authorisation code.

### 5.2 Using Non-Approved Chargers in Genuine Emergencies
- A "genuine emergency" is defined as: <10% SoC, no Symphony-approved station within 3 miles, AND Symphony Ops contacted FIRST.
- **Always call first, then plug in.** Retroactive approval is not guaranteed.
- Approved non-network exceptions in genuine emergencies: **Tesla Supercharger (v3 sites), ESB, Char.gy, local council on-street points** — but only with an authorisation code from Symphony Ops.
- Maximum reimbursed amount in an emergency: **£40 per session.** Anything above that requires Symphony Ops Manager sign-off (call, don't email).

### 5.3 Receipt Submission for Out-of-Network Charging
- Submit within **48 hours** of the session.
- Upload receipt + the **authorisation code** from Symphony Ops in the app.
- Without an authorisation code, the claim is treated as an unauthorised charge and **will not be reimbursed**.
